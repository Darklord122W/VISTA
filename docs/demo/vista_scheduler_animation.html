VISTA scheduler — interactive simulation

This is a simulation, not a measurement. It animates the real scheduling algorithm, but its service time and arrival rate are illustrative. Numbers shown here (including coverage) are directional only and are not the paper's results. The measurement archive behind the paper is not distributed with this repository.

A discrete-event simulation of the completion-clocked, bounded-stash batch former — the real algorithm (arrival → stash → gate → evict → score → top-K → completion credit), with illustrative timing, not the measured runs. Four ~30 fps cameras feed a per-camera stash; frames release only while the in-flight gate is open, and every dropped frame is counted. Watch where frames wait, age, and die.

Processed share per camera — concentration

Try it: turn importance off and watch service level out across cameras; turn it on and the active camera (cam 0) wins a larger share. Set d = 3 and the in-flight credit climbs to 6 — a standing queue re-forms (more latency, no throughput gain). Push K = 4 and every camera fits each batch, so the ranking stops mattering. Drag speed down to read individual decisions in the log. The exact cap-vs-concentrate magnitudes and the 42% coverage are artifacts of this sim's service time and arrival rate — directional, not the paper's measured numbers.
